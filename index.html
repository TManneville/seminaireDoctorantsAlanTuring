Seminary of PhD students of Alan Turing building

This seminary gathers PhD students of Alan Turing building: those of the LIX and of The INRIA Saclay. It is open to everybody.

There is a session more or less every two weeks. Any volunteer is welcome to give a talk between 30 and 45 minutes. The seminary aims at creating links between the students and let them know which topic are worked on in the Turing building. The talks are thus supposed to be introductory and reasonably accessible.

Calendar of the sessions

  • 14th session : 19/11/2015 at 3.00 pm. in room Flajolet Rémi Varloot sampling independent sets in a graph
    Abstract: In statistical physics, the possible states of a system are often represented as complex combinatorial objects. The microscopic interactions of the system define a distribution over these objects, from which its macroscopic properties are derived. Famous models include the Ising model, for ferromagnetic interactions, and the hard core gas model, for, well… gases. Numerical experiments require that one be able to generate random samples according to the corresponding distributions. This can be complicated, as these distributions are often defined up to a normalizing constant, which cannot be efficiently computed due to its combinatorial complexity. One means of overcoming this difficulty is the use of Glauber dynamics and the Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) method, which we will present in this talk. We also give a variant of this method, the coupling from the past (CFTP) algorithm, as well as some possible improvements.
